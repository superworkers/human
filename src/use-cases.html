Registered Investment Advisors

Compliance by architecture, not by hope.

Confidential national client.

The challenge

Human AI solution

Great Salt Lake Water Verification

Zero-trust orchestration for critical infrastructure.

The challenge

Utah is investing over $1B in Great Salt Lake conservation by 2034, but no integrated system exists to verify whether these investments deliver water to the lake. The RFP requires verifiable reductions from measurable telemetry, yet that infrastructure does not exist.

Water districts face an impossible choice: keep SCADA systems air-gapped and secure, or connect them and accept new attack vectors.

Human AI solution

  • Federated governance where each organization maintains data sovereignty.
  • Air-gap preservation through secure gateways that avoid SCADA exposure.
  • Cryptographic water receipts with SHA-256 hashing and ECDSA signatures.
  • Real-time water balance assessments with physics-informed modeling.

Project scope

  • $2.28M, 24-month initiative with 10-20 sensors at critical reaches.
  • Integrate 30+ existing USGS and Division of Water Rights gages.
  • Public dashboard tracking progress toward 2034 restoration goals.
  • 12,000-20,000 acre-feet/year in efficiency gains through optimization.

Partners

McCulley Watershed Consulting Services (Prime, Project Director), Human AI / Open Teams (technical infrastructure, cybersecurity, data science), Weber State University (groundwater modeling), University of Utah (isotope hydrology), Weber Basin Water Conservancy District (pilot deployment).

Why this matters for investors

Critical infrastructure requires zero-trust collaboration and court-admissible verification. The same architecture that protects water districts protects financial services firms from compliance exposure.
